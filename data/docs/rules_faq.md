# Rules & Qualification FAQ

This file is a lightweight reference for “rules” questions. When you ask a hybrid question (a rule plus a stat), the assistant can pull the rule from docs and the stat from SQL.

## Batting title qualification (typical rule of thumb)

Players generally need **502 plate appearances** to qualify for the batting title over a 162-game season (about 3.1 PA per team game).

If you ask a question like “Who led MLB in batting average in 1998?” you should specify whether you mean:
- Qualified hitters only, or
- All hitters regardless of plate appearances.

## ERA title qualification (typical rule of thumb)

Pitchers generally need **one inning pitched per team game** (about **162 innings**) to qualify for the ERA title.

## Quick clarifications

- Leaderboards can differ depending on minimum playing time filters.
- Team totals vs. player totals can differ if a player was traded mid-season; Lahman stores separate rows by team stint.
